Christos Noutsos, Ph.D.

Associate Professor of Biology, SUNY Old Westbury

I am an evolutionary genomicist and computational biologist studying how genomes are organized and how they evolve. My research spans the movement of organellar DNA into nuclear genomes (norgDNA), plant population genetics, transcription-factor gene families such as TUBBY/TLP, and the analysis of large-scale sequencing data. I combine bioinformatics, next-generation sequencing, and cloud computing to address questions from model to non-model species.

Alongside my research, I teach plant biology, bioinformatics, and genetics, and I mentor undergraduate researchers through course-based research experiences supported by the NSF.

Research focus

  • Genome organization & norgDNA — generation and evolutionary fate of organelle-DNA insertions in nuclear genomes of plants.
  • Population & ecological genomics — natural variation and differentiation in Aquilegia and other species.
  • Transcription-factor families — characterization of TUBBY (TLP) genes across crop and model species.
  • Bioinformatics & cyberinfrastructure — pipelines, cloud computing, and big-data analysis for plant biology.
