Research

Understanding how genomes are organized and how they evolve, from organelles to populations.

Nuclear organelle DNA (norgDNA)

A central thread of my work is the transfer of mitochondrial and chloroplast DNA into nuclear genomes. Beginning with my doctoral research on the generation and evolutionary fate of these insertions in flowering plants, I have shown how organellar DNA creates novel patches of exon sequence and contributes to genome evolution. Ongoing projects examine norgDNA across dozens of plant genomes and its relationship to pseudogenes and nuclear DNA content.

Population & ecological genomics

Using Aquilegia (columbine) as a system, I study gene flow, geographic and phenotypic differentiation, and the metabolomics of floral nectar. This work applies next-generation and third-generation sequencing to move population-genetic questions from model species into ecologically interesting non-model organisms.

Transcription-factor gene families

I characterize TUBBY-like (TLP) genes and their roles in seed germination and development across Zea mays, Triticum aestivum, Brachypodium, Oryza, Sorghum, and Arabidopsis, combining comparative genomics with developmental analysis.

Bioinformatics & cyberinfrastructure

As part of the iPlant Collaborative and through subsequent work, I build pipelines, tutorials, and cloud-computing workflows for large-scale plant genomics — integrating open-source tools, supporting collaborators' data analysis, and teaching reproducible big-data methods.
